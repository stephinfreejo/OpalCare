Vaccination

Vaccination is a crucial public health tool used to prevent the spread of infectious diseases. Vaccines work by stimulating the body's immune system to recognize and fight pathogens such as bacteria and viruses. They are one of the most effective means of preventing diseases and have been responsible for reducing the prevalence of many life-threatening illnesses. It has helped eradicate diseases like smallpox and has significantly reduced the incidence of others like polio and measles. Preventing diseases through vaccination reduces healthcare costs and prevents loss of productivity due to illness.

Childhood Vaccines

  • BCG (Bacillus Calmette-Gu√©rin) Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against tuberculosis (TB).

    Schedule: Given at birth or as soon as possible until 1 year of age.

  • Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV)

    Purpose: Protects against poliomyelitis (polio).

    Schedule: At birth, 6 weeks, 10 weeks, 14 weeks, and booster doses during national polio campaigns.

  • Hepatitis B Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against hepatitis B virus infection.

    Schedule: At birth (within 24 hours), 6 weeks, 10 weeks, and 14 weeks.

  • Pentavalent Vaccine (DPT + Hepatitis B + Hib)

    Purpose: Protects against diphtheria, pertussis (whooping cough), tetanus, hepatitis B, and Haemophilus influenzae type b infections.

    Schedule: 6 weeks, 10 weeks, and 14 weeks.

  • Rotavirus Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against rotavirus infections, which cause severe diarrhea in infants and young children.

    Schedule: 6 weeks, 10 weeks, and 14 weeks.

  • Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV)

    Purpose: Protects against pneumococcal infections, including pneumonia and meningitis.

    Schedule: 6 weeks, 10 weeks, and 9 months.

  • Measles and Rubella (MR) Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against measles and rubella.

    Schedule: 9 months and 15 months.

  • Japanese Encephalitis (JE) Vaccine (in endemic districts)

    Purpose: Protects against Japanese encephalitis.

    Schedule: 9 months and 16-24 months.

  • DPT Booster

    Purpose: Boosts protection against diphtheria, pertussis, and tetanus.

    Schedule: 16-24 months and 5-6 years.

  • OPV Booster

    Purpose: Boosts protection against poliomyelitis.

    Schedule: 16-24 months.

  • Vitamin A Supplementation

    Purpose: Prevents vitamin A deficiency, which can lead to blindness and increased mortality among children.

    Schedule: First dose at 9 months, followed by a dose every 6 months up to 5 years.

Adolescent and Adult Vaccines

  • Tetanus and Adult Diphtheria (Td) Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against tetanus and diphtheria.

    Schedule: Given at 10 years and 16 years of age. Also recommended for pregnant women during each pregnancy.

Additional Vaccines
  • Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against cervical cancer.

  • Influenza Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against seasonal flu.

  • Typhoid Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against typhoid fever.

  • Varicella (Chickenpox) Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against chickenpox.

  • Meningococcal Vaccine

    Purpose: Protects against meningococcal disease.
