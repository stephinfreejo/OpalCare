Low Carb Diet Plans

Keto Diet

The ketogenic diet is a high-fat, moderate-protein, and very low-carb diet. It aims to induce ketosis in the body, a metabolic state in which fat is used for fuel instead of carbohydrates.

  • Carbs: 5-10%
  • Protein: 20-25%
  • Fat: 70-75%

Foods:

  • Meat (beef, pork, chicken)
  • Fatty fish (salmon, trout, mackerel)
  • Eggs
  • Butter and cream
  • Cheese
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Healthy oils (olive oil, coconut oil)
  • Avocados
  • Low-carb vegetables (leafy greens, broccoli, cauliflower)

Atkins Diet

The Atkins Diet is one of the best-known low-carb diets. It focuses on reducing carbohydrates while emphasizing protein and fats to promote weight loss and improve overall health.

  • Carbs: 10-20%
  • Protein: 30-40%
  • Fat: 40-60%

Foods:

  • Meat (beef, pork, chicken)
  • Fatty fish (salmon, trout, mackerel)
  • Eggs
  • Low-carb vegetables (leafy greens, tomatoes, peppers)
  • Cheese
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Healthy fats (olive oil, butter)
  • Low-sugar fruits (berries)

Paleo Diet

The Paleo Diet is based on eating foods similar to those available in the Paleolithic era. It focuses on whole foods, lean proteins, vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds while avoiding processed foods, grains, and dairy.

  • Carbs: 20-30%
  • Protein: 30-40%
  • Fat: 30-40%

Foods:

  • Lean meats (grass-fed beef, chicken, turkey)
  • Fish and seafood
  • Fruits (apples, berries, citrus)
  • Vegetables (broccoli, spinach, carrots)
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Healthy fats (olive oil, coconut oil, avocado)
  • Eggs

Low-Carb Mediterranean Diet

This diet combines the principles of low-carb eating with the Mediterranean diet. It emphasizes consuming healthy fats, lean proteins, and vegetables while reducing the intake of carbohydrates.

  • Carbs: 10-20%
  • Protein: 25-35%
  • Fat: 45-55%

Foods:

  • Fish and seafood
  • Olive oil
  • Low-carb vegetables (spinach, kale, tomatoes)
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Avocados
  • Cheese
  • Yogurt
  • Poultry (chicken, turkey)
  • Eggs
